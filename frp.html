In-Depth Expert References

Sun X, Lam WK, Zhang X, Wang J, Fu W. Systematic review of the role of footwear constructions in running biomechanics: Implications for running related injury and performance. Journal of sports science & medicine. 2020;19(1):20-37.

  • This paper analyzes the science behind modern running shoes and how recent technologies impacted the rate of injury in runners. These new advancements include nylon and carbon fiber plates, an addition in shoes that has let elite athletes break historical records, albeit thought that the speed offered by these shoes may prolong injuries.

Saunders, Philo U, et al. “Factors Affecting Running Economy in Trained Distance Runners.” Sports Medicine, vol. 34, no. 7, 2004, pp. 465–485., doi:10.2165/00007256-200434070-00005.

  • This source investigates the connection between different physiological and biomechanical aspects of running economy in experienced distance runners. The studies conducted by the authors discovered that body mass, VO2max, lactate threshold, and stride length were all strongly related to running economy.

McArdle, William D., et al. Exercise Physiology: Nutrition, Energy, and Human Performance. Wolters Kluwer/Lippincott Williams & Wilkins, 2015.

  • Written by doctors in exercise physiology, this book takes an interest in the psychological aspects of exercise and running, taking on the “running is 90% mental and 10% physical” adage. This research has resulted in one of the most extensively used and regarded exercise physiology textbooks.

General Interest Resources

McDougall Christopher. Born to Run: A Hidden Tribe, Superathletes, and the Greatest Race the World Has Never Seen. 1st ed. Alfred A. Knopf; 2009.

  • This book delves into the science and culture of running, as well as the history of Mexico's Tarahumara People, who are famous for their long-distance running prowess. The book culminates with a 50-mile race between the Tarahumara and some of the world's greatest ultra-runners.

Parker, John L. Once a Runner. Cedarwinds Pub. Co., 1999.

  • The novel is a fictitious depiction of the life of a collegiate runner named Quenton Cassidy, and it is widely considered to be one of the finest running novels of all time.

Runner's World Magazine

  • This popular magazine covers a wide range of topics related to running, including training, nutrition, gear, and personal stories.

Eluid Kipchoge: Distance Legend

https://www.nnrunningteam.com/team/eliud-kipchoge/
Eliud Kipchoge is a world-renowned long-distance runner from Kenya who has won numerous marathons and Olympic medals. He has broken multiple historical records, most significantly being the first person to ever achieve the extraordinary sub-two hour marathon. He is widely seen as one of the greatest marathon runners of all time and redefined the limits of human biomechanics.

Roger Bannister: Breaking 4 Minutes

https://achievement.org/achiever/sir-roger-bannister-2
Roger Bannister was a British middle-distance runner who, on the Oxford track in 1954, became the first person to break the four-minute mile barrier, a feat long considered unthinkable. His historical achievement circled the globe and gained him worldwide recognition and fame, his accomplishment even being called “the greatest athletic achievement of the last 150 years” by Forbes in 2005.

David Wottle: 1972 Olympics

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5LHid-nC45k
David Wottle was an American middle-distance runner famous for an iconic come-from-behind victory, incredibly closing a four second, 20 meter gap in the 800 meters at the 1972 Olympics, winning the gold. A spectacular photo finish and famous for his signature golf cap, this race is still brought up 50 years later and is definitely worth the watch.

Online Resources

strava.com

Strava is a social network and smartphone app that allows users to log their fitness activities, especially running and cycling. Users can record and share their workouts, track their progress over time, and interact with other athletes using the app.

reddit.com/r/running

r/running is a subreddit dedicated to everything running. It is a global network of runners who share running information, stories, and tips. The subreddit contains a wide range of content, such as race reports, training schedules, and starting recommendations. Members also contribute running-related news and updates, as well as running-related events. I have to admit I find these millions of members being one of the kindest online communities available.

runnersworld.com

Once again, Runner's World is a well-known magazine and website that covers all elements of running. The magazine includes training, nutrition, gear, and race reviews, as well as profiles of great runners and inspirational stories about everyday runners. It was founded in 1966 and has quickly become one of the most well-known brands in the running industry, providing a wealth of tools and information to runners of all levels.
